LEAD 01 // TIME OBFUSCATION DETECTED

The Breakdown: Auditing the sale_date column revealed absolute chaos. Three distinct date formats in play: DD/MM/YYYY, YYYY-MM-DD, and raw Excel serial integers (like 44607). Standard time reports were broken.

The Sabotage Clue: More alarmingly, 12,400 rows had quarter values (like Q2) that contradicted the actual transaction month (e.g. July = Q3). If dates were deliberately mixed up, it would disguise weak sales quarters.

The Solution: Constructed Excel formulas using DATE, LEFT, MID, and RIGHT to parse serial numbers and standard formats, then derived clean, correct quarter flags.

LEAD 02 // GEOGRAPHIC SPLIT DETECTED

The Breakdown: The region column was splintered. What should have been 6 standard regions was written in 14 different ways. The North-West alone appeared as NW, North West, NORTH WEST, and north west.

The Sabotage Clue: Splitting regions masks distributor performance. Sales totals get divided, preventing managers from spotting when a specific territory is drastically underperforming.

The Solution: Built a clean mapping table and used nested IFS and UPPER checks to clean all 1,622 messy region rows into uniform boundaries.

LEAD 03 // PROMOTIONAL BLIND SPOT

The Breakdown: The promo indicator column was flooded with variants. A promo applied was Yes, YES, yes, Y, 1, and True. No promo was No, NO, N, 0, and False. Plus 13,094 rows left blank.

The Sabotage Clue: Bypassing standardized promo flags allows sales reps to issue unauthorized or hidden discounts, labeling them randomly so they escape audit checks.

The Solution: Cleaned all promo variants using logical Excel sweeps, mapping everything to a strict binary 1 or 0 indicator.

LEAD 04 // MISSING EVIDENCE

The Breakdown: Major gaps in key financial fields. 19,045 rows lacked a Total Revenue entry, and 7,664 rows lacked a Unit Price. Without these, total sales volume was heavily understated.

The Sabotage Clue: Blanks in revenue allow cash transactions to walk out the door. If a transaction occurred but the revenue was deleted, it's impossible to balance bank sheets against inventory depletion.

The Solution: Cross-referenced product SKUs against a master price sheet to populate missing unit prices, then computed missing revenue values mathematically.

LEAD 05 // THE DISAPPEARING NAIRA

The Breakdown: The most damning finding. In 14,447 rows, the recorded total_revenue_naira did not equal units_sold × unit_price_naira. The variance spanned millions of Naira.

The Sabotage Clue: This is a classic indicator of double-entry manipulation. Someone was overriding revenue formulas, potentially to report false performance figures or cover up inventory shrinkage.

The Solution: Wrote automated Excel verification checks to isolate mismatching rows and computed the true expected revenue for the audit report.

SUSPECT DOSSIER // MR. TUNDE

Role: Senior Commercial Analyst, Palmridge Account Lead

The Profile: Mr. Tunde was the sole custodian of the Palmridge sales sheet for the last two years. He designed the original reporting structures. Why He's Flagged:

  • He was the last user to access and save the CSV before the system logged a bulk database edit.
  • Multiple sales reps claim they reported region and discount discrepancies to him, but none of these reports were ever logged or resolved.

Investigation Verdict: Suspicious. Either he is hiding massive operational negligence, or he was actively cooperating with external parties to misreport sales performance. Investigation remains open.
